Appendix: Results validation with data from Lille miniMPL
In order to confirm the previous conclusions drawn from the Toulouse miniMPL dataset, the same tests
werecarriedoutonthedatafromtheLilleminiMPL.TheLilledatasetcontainsfarfewernightsavailable
for calibration than the Toulouse dataset (80 vs 211). This can be explained by the cloudier weather in
Lille and the sensitivity to false cloud detection.
The results show that, as with the Toulouse data, the correction adjusts the AOD to values closer to
the photometer measurements (Figure 15), and is therefore more accurate. It can also be noted that the
corrected AOD values are closer to the measured values than in the Toulouse case.
As expected, this also results in higher C values, with the median increasing from 222k to 266k
L
(Figures 16 and 17).
